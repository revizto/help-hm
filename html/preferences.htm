The Preferences screen has four views: General, Navigation, Graphics, Camera. Use them to customize your local instance of  Revizto. For project-level optimization options see the Project Optimization section.

To immediately see how your settings affect the model, turn on the Transparency switch. When the switch is on, the current view becomes translucent allowing to see the model through.

General

Normally, this view is the ending point of the installation process. Use it to define path to the working folder, set language preferences, measurement units and default view (i.e. the screen loaded  at launch).

Graphics

This view allows you to customize the quality of graphics and visualization mode. You can choose of one of the six modes; the recommended option is Good which compromises between response speed and visualization quality. The default quality option is selected by the built-in algorithm that evaluates your PC at the launch.

For each mode additional settings are available to fine-tune your visual experience. Basically, these are standard Unity effects.

SSAO and Realtime Shadows regulate the way shadows are rendered in the model.

Tone Mapping and HDR options allow modifying the dynamic ways. Tone Mapping is considered a less powerful tool.

By turning on/off SSAO, Realtime Shadows and Tone Mapping you can regulate the quality and type of shadows in the model. Note that SSAO is more taxing on the CPU capacity and Realtime Shadows take more RAM. It is recommended to switch off both or one of them, if PC capacity is low or model is too large.

Turn on Downscale rendering resolution if you are using a powerful high-resolution display with higher pixel density. Otherwise long delays are likely to occur. With this option on, Revizto will choose the nearest best resolution option to promptly render views combining adequate visualization and navigation speed.

Note that some of these settings are disabled when in 3D section cut/box mode.

Camera

This view allows you to customize camera positioning: how far you can see, how wide your field of view is, how high the camera is. The defaults match an average human height.  

Tips: Higher visibility distance can cause rippling shadows. To remedy for it, turn off the Realtime Shadows and SSAO in the Graphics tab. Turn Transparency on to make the current view translucent and see whether or not your settings are applied the way you want without quitting Preferences. For some preferences hot keys are available in most view modes.
